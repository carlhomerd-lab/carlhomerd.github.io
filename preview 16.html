ABOUT MCAI

The Mindanao College of Aeronautics Inc. (MCAI) is dedicated to producing world-class aviation professionals. Shaping Work, Mind, Hands, for future aviators. Located in the heart of Dipolog City, we provide rigorous technical training grounded in industry standards and hands-on experience.

VISION

To be the leading aviation technical institution in the region, fostering innovation, safety, and excellence in aircraft maintenance technology.

CAAP ACCREDITATION

Mindanao College of Aeronautics Inc. operates in full compliance with the standards set by the Civil Aviation Authority of the Philippines (CAAP), under ATOC NO. 2024-006, ensuring all curriculum and training facilities meet national aviation safety and technical requirements. CAMPUS & COMMUNITY

Experience life at Mindanao College of Aeronautics Inc. Our campus is designed to provide a comprehensive learning environment, combining theoretical knowledge with practical, hands-on experience.

Campus Facilities

  • Aviation Maintenance Hangar: Fully equipped for hands-on airframe and powerplant training.
  • Technical Library: Extensive collection of aviation manuals, regulations, and reference materials.
  • Modern Classrooms: Air-conditioned spaces integrated with multimedia learning tools.
  • Student Lounge: A dedicated space for students to relax, collaborate, and study.

Student Life & Organizations

At MCAI, we believe in shaping well-rounded aviation professionals. Students are encouraged to participate in various extracurricular activities to develop leadership, teamwork, and technical skills.

  • Aviation Mechanics Club: Engage in supplementary technical workshops and collaborative projects.
  • Student Council: Represent the student body and organize campus-wide events and initiatives.
  • PAF-ROTC Unit: Develop discipline, leadership, and a commitment to national service through our Philippine Air Force affiliated program.

Community Outreach & Industry Exposure

Industry exposure plays a key role in preparing our students for dynamic aviation careers. Our students actively participate in real-world technical events and site deployments to gain critical operations insights.

Aerotour 2025 — Mactan-Cebu International Airport

Students received direct technical exposure at the General Aviation Area (Aplus facility), checking actual operational workflows and ground support setups.

As part of this comprehensive field evaluation at the general aviation area, students closely explored emergency air transport service rotary wing aircraft to study their specialized engineering frameworks, safety protocols, and rapid-response mechanisms.
